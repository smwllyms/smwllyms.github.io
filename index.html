About Me

My driving force is to help create a virtual landscape where people of all backgrounds can enjoy an accessible and easy-to-use internet with web applications that make their lives easier. I am a strong proponent of open-source software and communities as well as for efforts to create educational programs and applications to help others learn.

My calling is to improve general knowledge on the nature of audio and music, specifically on how audio and music intersect to create pleasurable and effective listening experiences.

Education

I have earned B.S. (2022) and M.S. (2023) degrees in computer science from Virginia Tech. I was a member of the Galipatia engineering-focused living and learning community at VT. I also taught as an adjunct instructor for an introductory course to GUI, computer graphics and full-stack development in Spring 2024.

I was also a part of Virginia Tech's Distributed Virtual Environments (DVE) Lab that specializes in Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and eXtended Reality (XR). My Master's thesis, Exploring the Usability of Non-verbal Vocal Interaction (NVVI) and a Pitch-based Implementation explored the usability of non-verbal vocal interaction (NVVI) to control one-dimensional parameters, i.e., a slider. For example, imagine whistling to control the dimness of lights in your smart home, modulating the pitch upward to brighten and downward to darken.

Here are some of my publications and work I contributed on during my time at VT:

  • A Relative Pitch Based Approach to Non-verbal Vocal Interaction as a Continuous and One-Dimensional Controller
  • An Approach to Pitch Based Implementation of Non-verbal Vocal Interaction (NVVI)
  • Immersive Technology in the Public School Classroom: When a Class Meets
  • Redefining the Digital Paradigm for Virtual Museums

Mepository

Mepository is a virtual journal for you to record your feelings, achievements, and notable events in your life, allowing you to reflect, learn, and grow as a person all in one place. You are provided an interactive calendar to navigate and record your thoughts, feelings, and anything else you'd like to jot down. This app provides you somewhere accessible and easy to navigate to record and later reflect on your experiences through life, on a variety of temporal scales.
Check it out here

Fourier Analysis

Check out some of my work with the famous audio-processing Discrete and Fast Fourier Transform (DFT and FFT) algorithms! Explore an online calculator that showcases a variety of optomized algorithms for calculating FT and IFTs of input values, and visit the live fourier analysis where you can watch the algorithm at work as it guesses the pitch of oscillators or audio input from your microphone!
Check it out here

Audio Plugin Development

Explore audio processing in my plug-and-play web browser-based audio plugin GUI development sandbox! This work-in-progress is part of a larger project where I hope to exponentially improve VST development time by allowing prototyping in this sandbox. If you've ever tried developing VSTs using Steinberg's SDK, you know how time consuming and redundant much of it is.
Check it out here

x86 ASM Sandbox/Debugger


This online x86 assembly sandbox and debugger allows you to visualize and manipulate an x86 program's memory and stack during runtime. There is also a console that supports some functional operations including breakpoints (line and conditional), custom labelling, and more. Assembly from Ghidra can be directly imported (so long as the instructions are functional and label references are resolvable).
Check it out here

Image Convolution Sandbox

If you've ever wondered how some basic blurring and edge detection is implemented for images, feel free to explore this sandbox where you can upload your own images and convolve them with custom kernels and see what happens!
Check it out here

Music & Audio

I grew up playing guitar, the saxaphone, and french horn. Although I love many different types of genres of music, I have a special love for electronic dance music (EDM). It inspired me to begin producing music, a hobby I continue today. I first started using FL studio, my digital audio workstation (DAW), back when I was 13 years old. Over a decade later, I'm still at it. Additionally, this love for music helped aim me in the direction of audio programming, such as for my thesis (non-verbal vocal interaction) and coding audio plugins, mainly Virtual Studio Technology (VST) plugins.
